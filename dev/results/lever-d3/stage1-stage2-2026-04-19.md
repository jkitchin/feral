| `CscMatrix::to_dense`                  |  9.2 |            22.3 % |

| **dense_fast_factor total**            | **41.2** |           100 % |
| multifrontal oracle                    | 75.2 |             183 % |

(Phases overlap slightly because `compute_scaling` internally allocates,
which also appears in the end-to-end total; the ~0 synthesis row is
derived from total - sum-of-parts and is dominated by measurement
noise.)

**Interpretation.** The dense kernel itself takes 16 µs — within the
ex-ante budget. Densify + scale-apply add 10 µs. `compute_scaling`
alone costs 34 µs: **82 % of the dense-path wall time**. The
ex-ante TRO3X3 target (≤ 25 µs per `dev/research/sparse-tail-d3-d4-2026-04-19.md` §5)
is unreachable at the current scaling cost; even zero-cost routing
and factor would leave us at 34 µs.

This does *not* invalidate D.3 — the dense path still nearly halves
wall time (83 µs → 41 µs, −51 %) and wins 2-fold against MUMPS on
the matrix families the gate targets. But the real headroom is in
`compute_scaling` on small-dense inputs, not in the routing. That's
a separate follow-up (noted at the end).

### Crossover analysis

- **At ρ = 0.10**: dense wins n ≤ 96, ties at n = 128, loses at n ≥ 160.
  Tells us we *could* relax `ρ_min` below 0.25 at small n without
  regression. But at n = 128 it is a knife-edge (0.98×) and real
  matrices with delayed pivots can only slow it further. Keep
  `ρ_min ≥ 0.25` for safety.
- **At ρ = 0.25**: dense wins for all n in the swept range (up to 192),
  with 0.49–0.66× ratio. This is strong evidence we could widen
  `N_MAX` to 192 and still bank every matrix.
- **At ρ = 0.50 / 0.75**: dense crushes multifrontal 2–4× for
  n = 96–192. These are the "obvious wins" the gate is designed
  for.

### Tuning decision

**Keep `N_MAX = 128`, `ρ_MIN = 0.25`** (unchanged from the placeholder
thresholds committed in D.3 GREEN). Rationale:

- Synthetic diagonally-dominant matrices are the best case for BK;
  real IPM KKT matrices can be 1.5–2× slower under delayed pivoting.
  The sweep's 2-fold margin at ρ = 0.25 leaves room for that variance.
- Widening `N_MAX` to 192 would gate in matrices in the
  128 < n ≤ 192 band at ρ ≥ 0.25 — a small corpus slice. The
  corpus bench (stage 3) will confirm whether this slice exists
  and is worth capturing; if yes we re-open the threshold in a
  follow-up commit.
- The ρ floor stays at 0.25 because the 128 × 0.10 tie signals
  that lowering it risks regression the moment real matrices
  behave worse than the diagonally-dominant synthetic.

## Follow-up (out of D.3 scope)

- **`compute_scaling` on small-dense matrices.** The 82 % share on
  TRO3X3 is almost pure allocation + MC64/Auto dispatch overhead,
  not floating-point work. A small-n scaling fast path (or skipping
  scaling entirely for `density ≥ 0.5`, where BK pivots cleanly
  without conditioning help) could halve dense-path wall time.
  Separate plan; not authorized in D.3.
- **`CscMatrix::to_dense` allocates each call.** 9 µs on TRO3X3
  (22 % of dense total). Pooling the dense buffer via
  `FactorWorkspace` is the obvious next optimisation; mentioned in
  §"Interaction with FactorWorkspace" of the plan as a follow-up.
- **Stage 3 corpus bench** decides whether D.3 is "done" as-is or
  needs the two follow-ups above before closing.
