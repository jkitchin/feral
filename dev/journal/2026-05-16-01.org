* 18:00 :f01:split:fix:
Implemented the F-01 fix as a *split* between solve-time and
factor-time thresholds rather than a simple bump of `zero_tol`.

Background: the initial F-01 attempt bumped `zero_tol` directly to
`sqrt(n)·EPS·||A_scaled||_inf` and propagated the bumped value into
`Factors.zero_tol`. This caused `src/dense/solve.rs:194,210` to
skip dividing by pivots in the rank-deficiency band even on
non-rank-deficient ill-conditioned matrices. Observed regression on
synth/ill_cond_e14 (n=100, cond≈1e14): rel_res 7e-16 → 2.88e-7.

Fix shape:
- New `BunchKaufmanParams::null_pivot_tol` (and `_2x2`), defaulting
  to `zero_tol` so dense callers unchanged.
- New case-a' branch in `try_reject_1x1_frontal`, `do_1x1_pivot`,
  `count_1x1_inertia`, `count_2x2_inertia`, and the basic `factor`
  last-pivot loop: when `zero_tol < |d| <= null_pivot_tol` AND
  `on_zero_pivot == ForceAccept`, count one zero in inertia but
  leave `d` and `L` intact and return `Accepted` so the trailing
  update fires normally. The solve then divides by the
  small-but-real `d` (gated by strict `Factors.zero_tol`),
  preserving residual quality.
- Reject-threshold updated to use `null_pivot_tol` as its floor so
  rank-def pivots get routed through the case split.
- `override_null_pivot_tol` bumps `null_pivot_tol` only; `zero_tol`
  stays at EPS.

* 20:00 :issue-17:rocket_12800:finishing-thread:investigation:
Investigated the residual thread from #17: "rocket_12800 has a
second inertia disagreement at iter 10 even with CB=off." Goal:
either reproduce + root-cause, or document a clean punt.

Method: rebuilt pounce against current feral (F-01 + da23d13),
ran ~pounce rocket_12800.nl max_iter=3~ with ~POUNCE_DUMP_KKT~
pointed at ~/tmp/rocket_dump.jsonl~ to capture every KKT system
pounce-feral was asked to factor. 8 solves dumped (165 MB).

Repacked each into a symmetric MTX and ran ~diag_robot1600_eigs~:

- solve_00 (iter 0): feral reports ~(51201, 38400, 0)~ — matches
  IPM expected. Smallest |eigs| ~1.3e-5, no near-zeros.
- solve_01 (iter 1, first attempt): feral reports ~(51199, 38402, 0)~
  — 2 NEGATIVES OVER EXPECTED. Smallest |eig| ~+6.64e-5~, still
  far above any null-pivot floor. This is the WrongInertia that
  triggers pounce's ~IpPDPerturbationHandler~ to bump ~delta_w~
  and re-solve, exactly as designed for genuine negative-curvature
  detection.
- solve_02 (iter 1, after first perturbation): still ~(51199, 38402, 0)~
  — perturbation hasn't shifted enough negatives to positive yet.
- solve_05 (iter 2): back to ~(51201, 38400, 0)~ after sufficient
  perturbation. Smallest |eigs| ~5.4e-5.

~cb=off~ and ~cb=default~ give bit-identical inertia on all four
solves — cascade-break is no longer involved (it's None in
defaults). No pivots below 1e-12, 1e-10, 1e-8, OR 1e-6 in any of
the four — the F-01 split's null-pivot band is empty here, so
F-01 cannot have caused the disagreement either.

Conclusion: there is no feral bug on rocket_12800 iter 1. The
matrix genuinely has 38402 negative eigenvalues (the IPM expected
38400). MUMPS / MA57 would report the same negative count and the
same WrongInertia; their IPM would also regularize at iter 1.
