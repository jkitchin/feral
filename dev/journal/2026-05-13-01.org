* 11:00 :issue-9:step-3:design:
Studied =schur_panel_minus_nofma_strided_quad= signature and its
existing wiring in =apply_blocked_schur_panel=. The quad kernel's
per-element bit-exactness contract ("Bit-exact per column with four
sequential single-column rank-1 dispatches") is exactly what we need
for =update_1x1_block32= at n_elim=1. The natural Step 3 implementation
is a tile loop: 4-column tiles via _quad, 2-column trailer via _dual,
1-column trailer via =axpy_minus_unroll4_nofma=. The quad kernel was
added in Phase 2.4.3 specifically for issue #9 (docstring line 1227
"Phase 2.4.3 (issue #9, re-scoped)") — so this is the kernel the plan
was designed around.

Root cause: quad's per-q sequential =sub(sub(dst, m0), m1)= chain
differs from =axpy2_minus_unroll4_nofma='s fused =sub(dst, add(m0, m1))=
chain. The quad's bit-exactness contract is "with sequential rank-1
dispatches", not "with rank-2 axpy2". Reverted update_2x2_block32 to
the scalar per-column axpy2 body. Step 4 needs a custom 4-dst-column
2-src pulp dispatch with axpy2-style fused rounding; tracked in next-
session list.
