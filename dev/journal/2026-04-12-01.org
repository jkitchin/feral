* 09:10 :diagnosis:supernodes:
  Read src/symbolic/{mod.rs, supernode.rs} and src/numeric/factorize.rs.

  MGH10S has bordered structure: AMD perm is identity. Etree has roots
  {0,1,2, 35..50}; constraint row 35+i has children {3+i, 19+i} (each
  variable column couples to one constraint). The two children of any
  constraint subtree are *non-adjacent* in column index — separated by
  15 columns.

  find_supernodes amalgamates child-into-parent under size-based merging
  (all ncol < nemin=32). The merge tracks first_col = min(child, parent)
  in supernode.rs:175. After merging, "supernode 35" reports first_col=3,
  ncol=3 but its actual column SET is {3, 19, 35} — non-contiguous.

  Every downstream consumer treats first_col..first_col+ncol as the
  eliminated set:
  - supernode.rs:200: =row_indices = (first_col..first_col+nrow).collect()=
  - factorize.rs:130: =for (local_j, &gj) in row_indices[..ncol].iter()=
  - factorize.rs:253: =for j in first_col..first_col+ncol=

  So the frontal *meant* to eliminate {3, 19, 35} actually eliminates
  {3, 4, 5}, and the negative constraint diagonals at cols 35..50 are
  mostly never reached. By coincidence the snode lengths still sum to
  51 across the matrix, so column-coverage *looks* correct.

* 09:25 :root-cause:postorder:
  Found the orphan: src/ordering/postorder.rs exists with a working
  =postorder()= function and 5 tests, but is *never called* from
  symbolic_factorize. The original Phase 1b plan
  (dev/plans/phase-1b-sparse-solver.md:96) explicitly called for
  "Composing AMD perm with postorder perm gives the final ordering"
  but the implementation regressed against the plan.

  Without postorder, AMD-permuted etrees on KKT matrices with bordered
  structure have non-contiguous parent-child column relationships.
  Supernode amalgamation requires a postordered tree as a precondition;
  CHOLMOD's cholmod_analyze_p does
    Perm = AMD(A); Parent = etree(A(Perm,Perm)); Post = postorder(Parent);
    Perm = Perm[Post]; rebuild Parent and ColCount on the composed perm.

  Wrote dev/research/postorder-pipeline.md (~190 lines, with worked
  MGH10S example, CHOLMOD pseudocode, SSIDS reference, correctness
  invariants) and dev/plans/postorder-fix.md (12 steps, three
  regression tests, acceptance criteria). Commit 429507f.

* 10:05 :fix:postorder:
  Implemented CHOLMOD-style composition in src/symbolic/mod.rs::symbolic_factorize:
  1. AMD perm
  2. Build etree on AMD-permuted pattern (intermediate)
  3. Postorder the etree
  4. Compose: =perm[k] = amd_perm[post[k]]=
  5. Re-permute on the composed perm and rebuild etree
  6. Column counts on final pattern + etree
  7. Supernode detection on the postordered etree

  Change is ~15 lines (lines 66-104), one new import. The struct field
  =perm= is now the composed permutation rather than just AMD perm.
