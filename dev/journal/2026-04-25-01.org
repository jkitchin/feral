** Outlier root cause: PFIT2_0300

Trajectory of extended refinement (11 steps including initial):
=3.55e-6  3.55e-6  3.55e-6  3.55e-6  5.71e-8  2.89e-12  2.89e-12 ...=

Refinement plateaus for 4 steps then drops 8 orders to 2.89e-12 at
step 5. =solve_sparse_refined= uses a 2-strike stagnation rule
(=src/numeric/solve.rs:366=, =max_stagnant_steps = 2=). It exits at
step 3 — well before the basin transition at step 5. Production
returns 3.55e-6; production tolerance is 1.33e-9; **bug confirmed**.

Dense =solve_refined= also has a max_steps=10 loop but does not have
the same 2-strike rule (it tracks best-iterate but iterates the full
10), so dense converges normally.

** Bulk pattern: residual floor at ~3e-9 for n<10 matrices

The other 74 cases oscillate at a floor of 2-5e-9. Example trajectories:
- HS46_0376 (n=7): 6.60e-9 → 6.39 → 4.56 → 5.54 → 6.45 → 3.22 → 2.53 → 2.49 → 4.52 → 5.48 → 3.16 (best 2.49e-9, tol 1.55e-9, FAIL)
- FBRAIN3LS_0736 (n=6): 1.36e-8 → 3.72 → 3.93 → 2.51 → **2.99e-9** → 1.41 → 1.50 → 5.16 → 1.33 → 4.02 → 1.38 (best 2.99e-9, tol 1.33e-9, FAIL)

Even with 10 steps, several never reach the bench's =n*eps*1e6=
tolerance. This appears to be a refinement floor — supernodal
accumulation rounding noise on tiny matrices is ~3e-9, which is
2-3× above =n*eps*1e6= for n<10.

Some DO clear with extended IR: PFIT2_0300, CERI651DLS_0643,
ALLINITA_0750, HS114_0758. These would be saved by a less aggressive
stagnation rule. Most would not.

* 09:45 :tolerance:floor-noise:fix-candidates:
Two distinct fix paths:
  1. Relax =max_stagnant_steps= from 2 to 3 or 4 — saves PFIT2_0300
     and a handful of others, costs IR cycles on easy cases. Earlier
     2026-04-18-06.org tuning explicitly chose cap=2 over cap=3 to
     minimize IR cost on the 154k-matrix bench. Re-tuning needs to
     cost the regression.
  2. Adjust =residual_tol= from =n*eps*1e6= to =n*eps*5e6= or
     =4*n*eps*1e6= for n<16. The bench tolerance is independent of
     IR convergence; the IR floor for tiny KKT matrices is empirically
     ~3-5e-9 regardless of step budget. SSIDS sidecars likely use a
     similar n-scaled tolerance — should compare against
     =data/matrices/kkt/.../*.ssids.json= residuals.

CLAUDE.md hard rule: never loosen a test tolerance without human
approval. Do not change tolerance unilaterally. Document and ask.

Both fixes preserve correctness (factorization is right; IR is just
truncated or floor-limited). Neither is a sparse-pipeline algorithmic
bug.

Production exits 2 steps before the basin transition. With

=solve_refined=. Confirmed cause; fix is in the rule, not factor.

Side observation: dense and sparse produce *different valid*
factorizations.
- d_eq dense:  (8.4e-7, 9.2e-5, 1.2e-5, 1.5e3, 6.8e2, 5.7e2)
- scale sparse: (1.3e-7, 5.1e-5, 1.2e-5, 1.0e4, 4.5e3, 3.8e3)
- BK perm dense:  [0,1,3,2,4,5]
- BK perm sparse: [0,5,2,3,4,1]
- max |L_d - L_s| = 1.99, max |D_d - D_s| = 2.26

Both factorizations are correct (matching inertia (3,3,0); both
reach residual 2.89e-12 with enough IR). The bug is purely that
sparse IR truncates earlier.

Suggested fix shape (not implemented — costs retune):

#+begin_src rust
let near_floor = best_r_norm < 100.0 * threshold * b_norm;
if stagnant_count >= max_stagnant_steps && near_floor { break; }
#+end_src

Preserves 2026-04-18-06 IR-cost-control on easy cases (where the
plateau IS the floor) while letting ill-conditioned cases punch
through to the basin.
