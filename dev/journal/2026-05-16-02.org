* 11:30 :issue10:axpy:microbench:negative:
"Remaining lever" investigation per the issue-10 Phase 2 corpus
post-mortem: is the rank-1 trailing-update kernel
(`axpy_minus_unroll4_nofma`) overhead-bound at the small lengths
seen by 1D-banded Mittelmann supernodes (3..32)?

Built `src/bin/bench_axpy_small.rs` comparing three implementations
at lengths [3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 16, 32, 64, 128]:
  1. `pulp` — current `axpy_minus_unroll4_nofma` (SIMD + dispatch)
  2. `scalar` — plain `for (d,s) in dst.iter_mut().zip(src) { *d -= alpha * *s; }`
  3. `unroll4` — manual 4-way unroll without pulp dispatch

Result (min-of-5, 50M iters/measure):
#+begin_example
 len     pulp_ns   scalar_ns  unroll4_ns    pulp/scl     pulp/u4
   3        1.00        1.00        1.00       1.00x       1.00x
   4        1.00        1.00        3.00       1.00x       0.33x
   5        1.00        2.00        3.00       0.50x       0.33x
   6        1.00        2.00        4.00       0.50x       0.25x

  10        3.00        3.00        5.00       1.00x       0.60x

  32        5.00        4.00        6.00       1.25x       0.83x

Conclusion: pulp SIMD dispatch ties with plain scalar within 1ns/call
quantization at all small lengths. Manual unroll4 is /slower/. The
compiler auto-vectorizes the scalar form as well as the explicit SIMD
dispatch. This rules out kernel-call overhead as the bottleneck for
clnlbeam. Combined with the negative #33 SLB A/B and the negative #10
MAXFROMM Phase 2 A/B, the three architectural levers tried this session
all came up within noise on the 1D-banded Mittelmann panel.

Remaining levers for #10: (a) `Solver::with_ordering(ScotchND)` —
landed this session via #33 §3, untested empirically; (b) supernode
amalgamation (structural change to widen narrow leaves);
(c) acknowledge a hardware floor for sequential factorization.
