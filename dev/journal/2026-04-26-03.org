Three candidate per-supernode counts:

  (=nelim*(nelim-1)/2 + (nrow-nelim)*nelim=)

  (=nelim*(nelim+1)/2 + (nrow-nelim)*nelim=)

Results across 71 sampled matrices (15 families × ~5 indices each):

#+begin_example
=== Summary across 71 matrices with SSIDS sidecars ===
  C / SSIDS (current feral count):     geomean=1.914  median=1.833

  A / SSIDS (strict-lower only):       geomean=1.142  median=0.973

B/SSIDS median = 1.000 nails it: SSIDS counts the lower triangle
including the unit diagonal of the eliminated block. The strict-upper
triangle that =nrow*nelim= sweeps in is structurally zero and was
double-counting fill that doesn't exist.
