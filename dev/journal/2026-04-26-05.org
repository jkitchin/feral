* 23:10 :findings:
Sampled 6 expansion families (5 matrices each):
- DRUGDISE_0003: 1 pivot pos→neg flip, delta_w=0
- SSCOSINE_0000: *935 pivots* flipped pos→neg, delta_w=2.684e4
  (expected (5000,0,0), feral reports (4065, 935, 0))
- SSCOSINE_0002: 1 pivot pos→neg, delta_w=1.100e8
- EXPQUAD_0004: 1 pivot pos→neg, delta_w=0
- CHAINWOO/BROYDN7D/KTMODEL: 5/5 match in early iterations
- RAYBENDS_0008: 1 pivot pos→neg, delta_w=delta_c=1e-4

Two regimes:
1. *Off-by-1* on small-pivot iterations: ~1 pivot flips sign at the
   threshold. Likely a 1x1-vs-2x2 BK pivot decision boundary case.
2. *Bulk sign disagreement* on SSCOSINE_0000 (935 sign flips on a
   nominally SPD matrix). Residual still passes at ~1e-14.
   Suggests feral's BK pivot threshold treats some sequence of small
   pivots as 2x2 blocks with negative inertia contributions, while
   MUMPS uses 1x1 positive pivots. Both produce correct LDL^T but
   different sign labels for the eliminated block diagonal.

* 23:15 :conclusion:
The 837 sparse-only inertia mismatches are *accounting discrepancies,
not numerical failures*. Solution residual is unaffected (99.8% pass).
Two follow-up paths for next session:

a. Audit the 2x2-BK pivot accounting in
   =numeric/factorize.rs= — specifically whether the inertia
   contribution from each 2x2 block matches MUMPS's convention.
b. Consider reporting "absolute" inertia (count of nonzero pivots
   below/above threshold) separately from "BK 1x1/2x2 inertia"
   contribution. MUMPS's =INFOG(12)= reports the former.

For now, document and move on. Bench's "Inertia match" metric should
be qualified as "BK-convention inertia match" until the accounting
gap is closed.
