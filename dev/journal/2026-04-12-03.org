* 10:00 :scaling:convention:
Picked the pivot-order cache convention by reading
src/numeric/factorize.rs:69 and src/numeric/factorize.rs:217-248:

                let new_i = perm_inv[matrix.row_idx[k]];
                ... store at (new_i, new_j) ...

So after permute_csc_values, the permuted CSC is indexed in pivot
(new) positions. During assembly in factorize.rs Step 6, the
scattering loop will walk permuted.col_ptr[col] for col in pivot
space and read permuted.row_idx[k] in pivot space too. For each
such scatter we want:
    val * s_user[user_row] * s_user[user_col]
  = val * s_user[perm[new_row]] * s_user[perm[new_col]]
  = val * scaling_pivot_order[new_row] * scaling_pivot_order[new_col]
where scaling_pivot_order[k] := scaling_user[perm[k]].

`perm` in feral is new-to-old: perm[new_idx] = old_idx, which we
confirmed by reading the perm/perm_inv construction in
src/symbolic/mod.rs:88-92. So the cache is
    scaling_pivot_order: perm.iter().map(|&old| scaling_user[old]).collect()
which matches the direction in the plan exactly.
