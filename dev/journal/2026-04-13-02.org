New tests in tests/delayed_pivoting.rs (all pass):
1. factor_frontal_delays_first_pivot_when_may_delay: 4×4 frontal
   with ncol=2, tiny fully-summed block (1e-14) and strong trailing
   entries. Traced BK decisions by hand: gamma0=1.0 at r=2, but
   r>=ncol means r is not fully-summed, so the r-swap path is

   diag. With may_delay=true the kernel breaks on column 0:

   !needs_refinement, and contrib matches the raw 4×4 input.
2. factor_frontal_root_force_accepts_without_delay: same frontal

3. factor_frontal_partial_elim_with_delay: 5×5 ncol=4 block-
   diagonal split. Columns 0,1 (diag 2.0, 3.0) have gamma0=0 so

   trailing 3×3, so contrib equals the raw bottom-right submatrix —
   this is an independent oracle, not a self-check.

- Added a hard debug_assert that ff.n_delayed == 0 at the contrib-
  block store site. Today pivot_threshold=0.0 makes delays
  unreachable; Step 5 replaces the assert with the actual
  delay-aware scatter. Failing loudly if a delay sneaks through
  before Step 5 is much safer than silent misassembly via the
  old ncol-based row_indices[ncol..] slice, which does NOT
  account for the kernel's BK swaps within 0..ncol.

Why the assert matters: the kernel's perm array within 0..nelim
records the BK swaps, but the kernel's swaps can also touch
nelim..ncol (the delayed region) before it breaks. So taking
row_indices[nelim..ncol] as the delayed global indices would be
wrong — Step 5 must walk ff.perm to unpermute. For Step 4 the
assert makes this impossible to forget.

* 12:30 :phase-2.3:delayed-pivoting:bug:fixup:
Phase 2.3 Step 4 fixup: revert the `may_delay = !is_root` flag
flip and the `is_root` computation. Post-bench discovery —
running `cargo run --bin bench --release` panicked during the
sparse solver validation phase at src/numeric/factorize.rs:489:62
on `index out of bounds: the len is 6 but the index is 6`. The
panic fired inside `extend_add` because a child's ContribBlock
carried bogus row indices from a delay path I thought was
unreachable.

Root cause: the original plan and my Step 4 commit message
assumed delays could only fire when `pivot_threshold > 0`. Wrong.
The 2×2 Duff-Reid growth bound and the det-floor stability
checks in factor_frontal reject pivots based on eigenvalue
structure, independent of the column-relative threshold. With
may_delay=true those rejections return PivotOutcome::Delayed
and the kernel breaks early — producing n_delayed > 0 at the
store site, tripping the debug_assert in debug builds and
writing a garbage ContribBlock in release builds that extend_add
later dereferences out-of-bounds. Evidence: 7fb3779 panics on
SWOPF_0827, 29ccf83 (Steps 1-3) does not — pinpointing Step 4's
flag flip as the trigger.

Fix: deleted the is_root computation entirely and reverted the
call site to factor_frontal(.., ncol, false, params). Step 5
will re-introduce both is_root and the flag flip together with
the parent-side delay assembly, so the two pieces land
atomically. The NodeFactors.nelim, NodeFactors.n_delayed_in,
and ContribBlock.n_delayed fields — the purely additive data
model change — stay in place, so Step 4 is now strictly
data-model plumbing, matching the plan's Step 4 goal of
"no behavior change".

Lesson for Step 5: when flipping may_delay=true, audit EVERY
site that can return PivotOutcome::Delayed, not just the
threshold gate. The kernel has at least six such sites (1×1
fallback at k, 1×1 after r-swap, LAPACK-extension path,
last-resort 1×1, 2×2 growth-bound branch, 2×2 det-floor
branch). Step 5 must handle all of them, not just threshold.
