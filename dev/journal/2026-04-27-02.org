* 07:45 :findings:agent-mumps-result:
mumps-expert returned. Three highest-impact findings:

1. *Eliminate per-front allocations* (=numeric/factorize.rs:1114-1131=
   and =dense/factor.rs:734-739, 954-959=). Each front allocates two
   nrow*nrow Vecs and copies between them. For 1934 fronts of size 32
   on CHAINWOO this is ~16 MB of zero-fill + copy. MUMPS uses one
   global =S(LA)= arena. Expected: *2-3× kernel speedup*.

2. *Fuse scale + Schur update + argmax* in =do_1x1_pivot=
   (=dense/factor.rs:2050-2087=). Currently 2-pass; MUMPS pattern
   (=dfac_front_aux.F:1815-1822=) does 1-pass with fused MAXFROMM
   capture. Expected: *1.5-2× speedup* on the rank-1 hot loop.

3. *Lower =block_size= to ≤ max small-front size, or add tiny-front
   pulp axpy path* (=dense/factor.rs:949=). Current bs=64 + ncol≤bs
   short-circuit means CHAINWOO (max 32) never reaches the SIMD
   =axpy_minus= kernel. Expected: *1.3-1.6× speedup*.

Stacked: 4-6×. Plausibly closes the 5× per-nnz gap.

Research note: =dev/research/mumps-small-frontal-speed.md=.

* 07:50 :findings:agent-spral-result:
spral-expert returned. Three findings, partially overlapping with
mumps-expert (per-front allocation is the common point):

1. *Monomorphized 32×32 in-register LDL^T kernel* modeled on
   =block_ldlt<T,32>= in =block_ldlt.hxx:289=. Hand-rolled SIMD
   primitives: =find_maxloc= (vector tournament), =update_1x1=
   (4-way manually-unrolled FMA), =update_2x2= (=omp simd=).
   For a 32×32 aligned block the entire factorization is one
   inlined loop. Expected: *30-40% reduction in factor time*.

2. *Small-leaf-subtree batching* (=SymbolicSubtree.hxx:57-84=
   threshold 4·10^6 flops). Group leaves at analyse time, run as
   one OpenMP task with a single =lcol_= buffer for all nodes.
   Buddy allocator for contrib blocks. Expected: *15-25%* on
   chain-like graphs.

3. *Fused multi-column Schur kernel* (=axpyN_minus= for N=4),
   eliminating ~75% of pulp dispatch calls. Expected: *10-15%*.

Combined ceiling: ~50-60% reduction (89 ns/nnz → 35-45 ns/nnz),
landing feral in the SSIDS ballpark.
