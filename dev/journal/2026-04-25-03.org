#+TITLE: 2026-04-25 Session 03 — Phase 2.10 supernode profiler

* 14:00 :phase-2.10:profiler:plan:
Following the multi-agent investigation of the tiny-IPM tail (recorded
in 2026-04-25-01.org and -02.org), wrote the Phase 2.10 plan at
=dev/plans/phase-2.10-supernode-profiler.md=. The plan: a
zero-overhead-when-None per-supernode profiler that decomposes
=factor_us= into prologue + per-supernode loop + epilogue, with the
loop bucketed by front =nrow= (≤8, 9-16, 17-32, 33-64, 65-128, >128).
Goal: settle whether the MUMPS gap on tiny-IPM matrices is amalgamation
(many tiny fronts) or kernel (a few big fronts).

* 15:30 :phase-2.10:binary:result:
Added =src/bin/profile_supernode_distribution.rs=: 5-run median over
{ACOPR30_0067, CRESC100_0000, LAKES_0000, NELSON_0000, SWOPF_0000}
with warm-up and =FactorWorkspace= reuse. Output strongly validates
the amalgamation hypothesis:

| matrix         | snodes | ≤8        | 9-16 | 17-32 | 33-64 | loop_us | overhead% |
|----------------+--------+-----------+------+-------+-------+---------+-----------|
| ACOPR30_0067   |    341 | 332 (97%) |    8 |     1 |     0 |    1060 |       5.4 |
| CRESC100_0000  |    600 | 599 (99%) |    0 |     1 |     0 |    1003 |     ~23.0 |
| LAKES_0000     |    216 | 214 (99%) |    1 |     0 |     1 |     340 |     ~21.3 |
| *NELSON_0000*  |    256 | 256 (100%)|    0 |     0 |     0 |      98 |     ~48.7 |
| SWOPF_0000     |     42 | 38 (90%)  |    2 |     2 |     0 |     ~83 |       big |

NELSON_0000 is the most extreme: 100% of supernodes are ≤8 columns
wide. =overhead_pct= for the small matrices is dominated by
prologue+epilogue allocation/setup, not by big-kernel work.

SWOPF_0000 is bimodal: tiny fronts contribute 4.7% of loop time,
the 4 medium fronts (17-32 + 9-16) carry 95%. This is the exception
that proves we should look at SWOPF's medium-front kernel separately.

* 16:00 :phase-2.10:conclusion:
Conclusion: the tiny-IPM tail is overwhelmingly an *amalgamation*
problem, not a kernel problem. Closing this gap means either
(a) an aggressive small-front merge pass at symbolic-factorization
time (mirroring MUMPS's KEEP(197)=1 + NEMIN=5), or (b) a fast-path
kernel that avoids per-front allocator round-trips when nrow ≤ N
(mirroring MUMPS's NASS<24 single-shot path). Phase 2.11 should
prototype (a) first because it is closer to MUMPS's actual behavior
and the histogram suggests the wins are large.

Acceptance invariants for Phase 2.10 satisfied:
- count_sum == n_supernodes (no warnings)
- bucket sum_us == loop_us (no warnings)
- prologue + loop + epilogue ≤ total_us across all 5 matrices

* 17:30 :phase-2.11:diagnostic:

tree shape on the tail. Result: every tail matrix is bushy. NELSON
has *one* parent with 129 children and 0 other multi-child nodes;
CRESC100 has 189 multi-child internal nodes (100% of internal
nodes); ACOPR30 has 14 (13%); LAKES has 34 (97%); SWOPF has 6
(85%). The "estimated sibling-merges blocked by the adjacency
check at =supernode.rs:204-236=" equals leaf count on every tail
matrix.

Step A added counters to identify which =find_small_leaf_groups=
breaker dominates: arena overflow vs nrow_max rejection vs
non-qualifying snode. Result: every tail matrix flushes 100% of
its groups due to *non-qualifying snodes interleaved between
leaves in postorder*. arena_budget never fires (nrow_actual p95 is
2-10 across tail). nrow_max rejection never fires (qualifying ==
leaves on every matrix).

* 19:00 :phase-2.12:decision:
Phase 2.12 decision gate run. Reproducibly measured 60-67% factor-time
reduction on tail matrices (ACOPR30/CRESC100/LAKES) and 2-3× supernode
reduction on every tiny-IPM tail matrix. But the corpus bench
(=cargo run --release --bin bench=) showed:

| ratio                       | Adjacency | Renumber | Δ     |
|-----------------------------+-----------+----------+-------|
| sparse factor/MUMPS p50     | 0.30      | 0.33     | +10%  |
| sparse factor/MUMPS p90     | 1.70      | 1.89     | +11%  |
| sparse factor/MUMPS p99     | 3.79      | 3.45     | -9%   |
| sparse factor/MUMPS max     | 11.36     | 10.64    | -6%   |
| sparse small-frontal p90    | 1.69      | 1.88     | +11%  |
| sparse medium p90           | 1.70      | 1.89     | +11%  |

Plan's hard graduation criterion #4: corpus median total_us within ±5%.
The +10% p50 / +11% p90 regression on the long tail of small matrices
exceeds that budget. Per the plan branch-on-failure: keep =Renumber=
opt-in, do not flip default. Adjacency stays default.

Top-10 worst sparse factor ratios under Adjacency (ACOPR30 ≈ 11×,
CRESC100 ≈ 10×) move out of the worst bucket entirely under Renumber
— now KIRBY2_*/MUONSINE_* dominate the new top-10 (max 10.64×). The
Renumber tail is genuinely shorter; the median just gets slightly
heavier per matrix, presumably because the wider supernodes feed
denser kernels that don't amortize on tiny matrices.

Phase 2.12 lands as an opt-in feature with a decision-log entry. The
follow-up is a shape-dispatched =AmalgamationStrategy::Auto= (parallel
to =OrderingPreprocess::Auto=) — Phase 2.13+.
