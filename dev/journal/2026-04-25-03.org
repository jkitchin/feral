in 2026-04-25-01.org and -02.org), wrote the Phase 2.10 plan at

the 4 medium fronts (17-32 + 9-16) carry 95%. This is the exception
that proves we should look at SWOPF's medium-front kernel separately.

* 16:00 :phase-2.10:conclusion:
Conclusion: the tiny-IPM tail is overwhelmingly an *amalgamation*
problem, not a kernel problem. Closing this gap means either
(a) an aggressive small-front merge pass at symbolic-factorization
time (mirroring MUMPS's KEEP(197)=1 + NEMIN=5), or (b) a fast-path
kernel that avoids per-front allocator round-trips when nrow ≤ N
(mirroring MUMPS's NASS<24 single-shot path). Phase 2.11 should
prototype (a) first because it is closer to MUMPS's actual behavior
and the histogram suggests the wins are large.

Acceptance invariants for Phase 2.10 satisfied:
- count_sum == n_supernodes (no warnings)
- bucket sum_us == loop_us (no warnings)
- prologue + loop + epilogue ≤ total_us across all 5 matrices

expected (a)-(e) shortlist cleanly:

1. =KIRBY2_0007= (n=458, ratio 9.5x MUMPS): numeric phase is only

   + Renumber + small-leaf grouping) takes 924µs, 6x MUMPS's entire

   the apparent gap is analyze-phase overhead, not kernel.

2. =MUONSINE_0000= (n=1537): under Adjacency, ratio is 1.4x MUMPS
   (513 ncol≤5 fronts, numeric 332µs). Under Renumber default,
   ratio is 5.5x — Renumber merged a path-like etree into a single
   ncol=32 root frontal that costs 1008µs by itself. *Renumber
   actively regressed this family* by over-merging a tree shape
   that didn't need merging.

Decision (with user): stay with Renumber default; queue both as
Phase 2.13 work. The MUONSINE regression argues for shape-
dispatched =AmalgamationStrategy::Auto= (Phase 2.13a); the KIRBY2
finding argues for symbolic-phase profiling and per-call setup-cost
reduction (Phase 2.13b), independent of amalgamation strategy.

Conclusion: *AMD ordering is the dominant symbolic-phase cost on
small-n KIRBY2 (85.5%) and the largest contributor on MUONSINE
(46.9%).* The 924µs symbolic finding from the earlier diagnostic
is roughly 770µs AMD + 130µs everything else.

This refines Phase 2.13b's fix space considerably:
- (i) symbolic caching across same-pattern factorizations remains
  the biggest win — IPM iterations re-pay all 14 stages on every
  call when only numeric values change.
- (ii) "skip-on-small-n" cannot skip AMD; correctness depends on
  the ordering. The targets for skip-style optimization are tiny
  stages (postorder, find_supernodes) that aren't where the cost
  is.
- (iii) shrinking AMD's per-call constant is the realistic single-
  stage win. =feral-amd= per-call setup (allocations, hash table
  sizing for n=458) is the suspect; it deserves its own probe.

Bench fairness question stays open: MUMPS's reported factor_us
likely includes its own analyze. If MUMPS's AMD on n=458 is
microseconds rather than 770µs, that's a feral-amd quality issue,
not a fairness issue.

Plan adjustment: Phase 2.13b step 2 ("confirm hypothesis") is
done — the hypothesis is confirmed and refined to "AMD per-call
setup cost dominates on small-n". Step 3 (bench methodology
verification) and the fix decision (caching vs AMD constant
