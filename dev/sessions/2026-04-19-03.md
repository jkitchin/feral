### Policy 4: Auto fallback to InfNorm (commit `af9315d`)

`compute_scaling(&Auto)` runs a cheap post-scaling diagnostic
when `pick_scaling_strategy` would route to MC64. Three
conditions must ALL fire to fall back to InfNorm:

1. `raw_diag_range(matrix) < 1e6` — raw matrix is well-behaved.
2. `mc_off > 1e6` — MC64's scaled `max(|off|/|diag|)` is large.
3. `mc_off / in_off > 1e5` — MC64 catastrophically worse
   than InfNorm.

The first guard (raw_diag_range) was added during landing
after the original 2-condition rule false-positived on the
MEYER3NE parity matrices (raw_drng ≈ 1e19, where MC64 is
genuinely needed even though scaled mc_off looks bad).

A second bug found during landing: the fallback returned
`ScalingInfo::NotApplied` initially, which made
`numeric::solve` skip pre/post-scaling entirely. InfNorm's
actual return is `ScalingInfo::Applied`. Forwarded the
correct info; MSS1_0007–0013 went from residual ≈ 2.4e-3
back to 6e-12.

### Sparse-tail perf survey + Lever D.2 ruled out (commit `8e68482`)

`dev/research/sparse-tail-perf-2026-04-19.md` documents the
post-Policy 4 sparse perf state and surveys the top-10 worst
factor-ratio matrices. Three outlier classes:

- **Class A (tiny, n ≤ 9):** HAHN1LS_0429, CHWIRUT2LS_0184,
  FBRAIN3LS_0003, PALMER5B_0049 — ~100 µs flat overhead vs
  MUMPS's ~10 µs.
- **Class B (small-medium, n=69-645):** LAKES_1199,
  TRO3X3_0013, CHWIRUT1_0009, KIRBY2_0006/7.
- **Class C (large arrow-KKT):** MUONSINE_0000 — already at
  the lever-C floor.

Per-family geomean tells a different story: AVION2 (geomean
1.61, 2682 matrices) and BATCH (1.85, 2054 matrices) lose to
MUMPS on average. ~7700 matrices = 5% of corpus where geomean
> 1. Pulling those down would shift corpus geomean visibly.

**D.2 (per-family analysis on AVION2/BATCH) attempted in this
session.** Profiled AVION2 and BATCH at `nemin ∈ {1, 5, 32}`
via `profile_sparse` with a `FERAL_NEMIN` env-var override:

| matrix       | n   | nnz | fac µs nemin=32 | nemin=5 | nemin=1 |
|--------------|----:|----:|----------------:|--------:|--------:|
| AVION2_0000  |  94 | 167 |              35 |      33 |      48 |

| BATCH_0000   | 121 | 299 |              80 |      82 |      92 |
| BATCH_0500   | 121 | 305 |              72 |      83 |     103 |

`nemin=32` (current default) is at the optimum. Smaller

multifrontal scaffolding overhead, not a tunable.

**Updated recommendation: Lever D.1 (FactorWorkspace arena).**
Per-call allocations (frontal matrices, assembly index maps,
pivot sequences as fresh `Vec`s per supernode) likely dominate
small-matrix factor cost. The `Solver` struct already caches
`SymbolicFactorization` across IPM iterations; extending it
to numeric scratch is the natural follow-on. Concrete next
step: alloc instrumentation on AVION2_0000/BATCH_0000 to
confirm the hypothesis before designing the API.

`profile_sparse` was extended in-session with the eight
top-10 outliers plus AVION2/BATCH/HS118 samples for future
profiling work.

## Decisions Made

1. **Policy 4 fallback rule shipped** with three conditions
   (raw_diag_range < 1e6 ∧ mc_off > 1e6 ∧ mc_off / in_off >
   1e5). Inline in `compute_scaling_auto`, no public API
   change. See `dev/decisions.md` entry 2026-04-19 (this
   session).

2. **`ScalingInfo::Applied` is the contract for any non-trivial
   scaling vector.** `NotApplied` is reserved for the cases
   where the returned vector should NOT be applied
   (Identity / External). `numeric::solve` keys off this to
   skip pre/post-scaling. The Policy 4 landing bug confirmed
   this is a load-bearing invariant. See `dev/decisions.md`
   entry.

## Abandoned Approaches

1. **2-condition Policy 4 rule** (mc_off > 1e6 ∧ mc_off/in_off
   > 1e5, no raw_diag_range guard). False-positived on
   MEYER3NE_{0220, 0259, 0253} parity tests (raw_drng ≈ 1e19,
   where MC64 is genuinely the right scaling). Replaced with
   3-condition rule before landing.

2. **`ScalingInfo::NotApplied` for Policy 4 fallback path.**
   Initial implementation; made solve skip pre/post-scaling,
   regressing MSS1_0007–0013 to residual ≈ 2.4e-3. Replaced
   with `ScalingInfo::Applied` (forwarded from
   `infnorm::compute_infnorm`).

3. **Lever D.2: `nemin` tuning to fix AVION2/BATCH families.**
   Tested `nemin ∈ {1, 5, 32}` via `FERAL_NEMIN` override on
   `profile_sparse`. Default `nemin=32` is optimal; smaller
   values regress. The AVION2/BATCH gap is multifrontal
   scaffolding overhead at small n, not amalgamation policy.
   Lever D.1 (workspace reuse) is the right next attempt.
