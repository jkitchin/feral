## Accomplished

### (1) SSIDS sparsity gap — RESOLVED (counting artifact)

- Wrote `src/bin/diag_factor_nnz_accounting.rs` to audit feral's
  `factor_nnz()` against MUMPS `INFOG(9)` and SSIDS `inform%num_factor`.
- Three candidate per-supernode counts compared across 71 matrices
  (15 families × ~5 indices each). Result:
  ```
  C / SSIDS (current `nrow*nelim`):    geomean=1.914  median=1.833
  B / SSIDS (lower-tri inc diag):      geomean=1.149  median=1.000
  A / SSIDS (strict-lower only):       geomean=1.142  median=0.973
  ```
  B/SSIDS median = 1.000 confirms SSIDS counts the lower triangle
  including the unit diagonal of the eliminated block.
- The previous `nrow * nelim` count swept in the strict-upper triangle
  of the eliminated block (structurally always zero). That was the
  source of the 1.75× gap.
- Updated `SparseFactors::factor_nnz()` and the `summary()` accumulator
  in `src/numeric/factorize.rs` to compute
  `nelim*(nelim+1)/2 + (nrow-nelim)*nelim` per supernode. 333 tests
  pass. Committed as `ae81b81`.
- Bench rerun on `kkt` corpus confirms:
  - **nnzL/SSIDS: p50 1.75 → 1.00** (median parity). p99 8.00 → 4.50.
    Max 9.00 → 5.00.
  - nnzL/MUMPS p50 1.00 → 0.62 — MUMPS's `INFOG(9)` inflates with
    delayed-pivot/pre-allocation overhead, so feral being smaller than
    MUMPS by INFOG(9)'s definition is a property of MUMPS's reporting,
    not a feral regression.
  - Residual / inertia results unchanged (99.8% sparse residual pass).
