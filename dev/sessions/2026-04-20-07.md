Mixed picture. The two worst-of-worst (MUONSINE, VESUVIA) got
significantly faster. Several mid-tail matrices regressed: CRESC100
+2.48, KIRBY2 family +0.4–0.6, VESUVIO family +0.1–0.9. This is
expected — the blocked kernel's Schur complement is hand-rolled
axpy-unroll-4 without SIMD (Phase 2.4.2 target), so for fronts that

panel overhead can dominate.

The SPRAL/MUMPS references get their speed from SIMD-vectorized
GEMM; feral's Step 4b/5 work is the blocking skeleton, and Phase
2.4.2 is the SIMD layer on top. So the wire-up is a **correctness
milestone** for the blocking plumbing — arrow-KKT tail validated to
use the panel path without regressions in verdict — but the full
perf win is gated on SIMD.

## Decisions Made

- **Single wire-up point**: replace `factor_frontal` call-sites with
  `factor_frontal_blocked`. The fallback-to-scalar gate inside
  `factor_frontal_blocked` makes this a drop-in replacement with zero
  risk for small frontals.
- **No per-front dispatch heuristic yet**: the blocked kernel's
  internal `bs < 2 || ncol <= bs` gate is sufficient. A more
  sophisticated "only block if `nrow - k > bs AND contribution-block
  cost > panel overhead`" heuristic may be needed once SIMD lands
  (Phase 2.4.2), but today's bench shows verdicts all PASS so it's
  premature.
- **Keep `factor_frontal` public**: it remains the canonical scalar
  kernel and the fallback target. Users who don't want panel
  overhead can still call it directly.
