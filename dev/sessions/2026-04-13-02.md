## Decisions Made

None that alter `dev/decisions.md`. Two implementation choices
worth recording in the journal only:

1. **Combined Steps 1-3 into one commit** rather than a pure
   red-phase first commit, because the failing tests cannot
   compile without the `may_delay` parameter and the `nelim`
   field. A pure red commit would leave `cargo test` broken
   across revisions — unacceptable.
2. **Deferred `is_root` and the `may_delay = !is_root` flag
   flip to Step 5's commit** rather than landing them in
   Step 4, because 2×2 Duff-Reid and det-floor pivot
   rejections can fire `Delayed` independently of
   `pivot_threshold`. Enabling `may_delay` before the parent-
   side assembly is ready leads to out-of-bounds in
   `extend_add` on matrices with naturally unstable 2×2
   candidates (SWOPF family). Steps 4 and 5 must flip the
   flag atomically with the parent-side unpermutation scatter.

## Abandoned Approaches

**`may_delay = !is_root[snode_idx]` as a plumbing-only
change in Step 4.** Introduced in commit `7fb3779`, reverted
in commit `c84b7c9`. Symptom: `cargo run --bin bench --release`
panicked during sparse solver validation at
`src/numeric/factorize.rs:489:62`:

    thread 'main' panicked at 'index out of bounds: the len
    is 6 but the index is 6'
    stack backtrace:
      ...
      3: feral::numeric::factorize::factorize_multifrontal
      4: bench::main

The panic is a global-index overflow inside `extend_add`
triggered by a child's `ContribBlock` carrying bogus
`row_indices` on SWOPF_0827. Root cause: the plan's
"delays can only fire when `pivot_threshold > 0`" assumption
is wrong. The 2×2 Duff-Reid growth bound and the det-floor
stability checks reject pivots based on eigenvalue structure,
independent of the column-relative threshold. With
`may_delay = true` those rejections return
`PivotOutcome::Delayed` and the kernel breaks early —
producing `n_delayed > 0` at the store site, tripping the
`debug_assert(n_delayed == 0)` in debug builds and writing a
garbage `ContribBlock` in release builds.

Evidence pinpoint: commit `7fb3779` reproduces the panic on
SWOPF_0827; commit `29ccf83` (Steps 1-3 only) does not.

Lesson logged: when Step 5 flips `may_delay=true` for
non-root supernodes, audit **every** site that can return
`PivotOutcome::Delayed`, not just the threshold gate. The
kernel has six such sites: the 1×1 fallback at `k`, the 1×1
after `r`-swap, the LAPACK-extension path, the last-resort
1×1, the 2×2 growth-bound branch, and the 2×2 det-floor
branch.

Not appended to `dev/tried-and-rejected.md` because this is a
sequencing mistake, not an abandoned approach — the flag flip
is still correct, just must land atomically with Step 5's
parent-side assembly.
