### 3. `probe_issue_19` binary + robot_1600 measurement

New `src/bin/probe_issue_19.rs` times `Solver::factor()` under
three configs on a single KKT mtx (sequential, parallel-with-gate,
parallel-with-gate-off) and reports the gate's decision.

Ran on `data/matrices/kkt-mittelmann/robot_1600/` iters 0000,
0001, 0003, 0006 (30 reps each):

| iter | est_flops | seq (med) | gated | forced-par | par speedup |
| ---- | --------- | ---- | ---- | ---- | ---- |
| 0000 | 4.75e6 | 5.24 ms | 5.26 ms | 7.87 ms | **0.67×** (hurts) |
| 0001 | 1.13e7 | 9.65 ms | 9.62 ms | 6.80 ms | **1.42×** |
| 0003 | 1.13e7 | 10.27 ms | 10.29 ms | 6.96 ms | **1.48×** |
| 0006 | 9.43e6 | 8.31 ms | 8.19 ms | 6.70 ms | **1.24×** |

Findings:

1. **The original issue #19 12× wall regression does not reproduce
   on feral-home post-`91e028a`.** Forced-parallel is faster than
   sequential on 3/4 iters. ThreadPool reuse alone closed the
   regression.
2. **Same crossover at ~6×10⁶ flops on robot_1600 as on
   Poisson-KKT.** Iter 0000 (below break-even) → parallel hurts;
   the rest (above break-even) → parallel wins 1.24-1.48×.
3. **The old `PAR_MIN_FLOPS = 1e8` was blocking all three
   robot_1600 wins.** Even the session-05 recommendation of 3e7
   would have blocked iters 0001/0003 (1.13e7).

### 4. Lowered `PAR_MIN_FLOPS` from 1e8 to 1e7

Commit `b12e03c`. Trade-offs:
- iter 0000 (4.75e6 < 1e7) → stays sequential ✓
- iter 0001/0003 (1.13e7 > 1e7) → now parallel ✓
- iter 0006 (9.43e6 < 1e7) → still sequential; sacrificed 1.24×
  win for ~1.6× safety margin above the 6e6 break-even.

This revises the session-05 research-note's 3e7 recommendation.
Justification: 30-rep direct measurement on the actual issue
workload (robot_1600) shows min/p90 of parallel well separated
from sequential's min at 1.13e7 flops, outside the noise band
that motivated the conservative 3e7. The K=50 Poisson-KKT 1.2×
win that drove 3e7 was an outlier of measurement timing, not the
rule.

## Decisions Made

- **`PAR_MIN_FLOPS = 10⁷`** is the new default. Calibrated on M4
  Pro from direct measurement on `robot_1600` KKT dumps. Above
  iter 0000's 4.75×10⁶ (parallel hurts 0.67×) and at or below
  iters 0001/0003's 1.13×10⁷ (parallel wins 1.4-1.5×).
- **Issue #19 is resolved-by-ThreadPool-reuse (`91e028a`)**, not
  by the gate. The threshold change is a secondary fix: the gate
  was leaving wins on the table by being too conservative.
- **The work-aware gate stays.** It correctly vetoes parallel on
  iter 0000-class problems (4.75×10⁶ flops, parallel hurts 33%).
  The gate's job is no longer regression-prevention against
  cv-wait but break-even tracking.
