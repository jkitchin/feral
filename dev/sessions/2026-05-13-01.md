## Decisions Made

- **Phase C scope narrowed to contrib-only single-slot.** Lifecycle
  analysis of `FrontalFactors` fields: only `contrib` has a natural
  recycle point (post-extend_add). The other fields (`l`, `d_diag`,
  etc.) move into long-lived `NodeFactors`, with no recycle point
  during factorisation. Pooling them would need an ABI break or
  `take_into_factors` swap; deferred indefinitely.
- **Issue #13 re-scoped: criterion #2 unreachable via this lever.**
  Per-front kernel cost (32×32 SIMD, issue #9) is the next plausible
  lever for the bench-ratio gap.
- **Step 4 of #9 requires a custom rank-2 4-dst kernel.** The quad
  kernel cannot be reused at n_elim=2 because its per-q
  `sub(sub(dst, m0), m1)` chain differs by 1 ULP from
  `axpy2_minus_unroll4_nofma`'s `sub(dst, add(m0, m1))`. A fresh
  `schur_kernel` function (e.g. `axpy2_dst4_minus_nofma`) with
  axpy2-style fused rounding chain across 4 dst columns is the
  Step 4 deliverable.

## Abandoned Approaches

- **Phase C multi-slot (`Vec<Vec<f64>>`).** Stack-of-buffers
  recycle preserved bit-parity but regressed bench p90 by ~+0.19
  small / ~+0.30 medium. Growable-indirection bookkeeping cost
  exceeded malloc/free savings. Replaced by single-slot. Recorded
  in `dev/tried-and-rejected.md` 2026-05-13 entry.
- **Issue #9 Step 4 via quad kernel at n_elim=2.** Tried tiling
  4 dst columns through `schur_panel_minus_nofma_strided_quad`
  with `n_elim=2` (i.e., both source columns of the 2×2 pivot
  block). Failed bit-parity tests at p=0 and p=10/p=28 with 1-ULP
  divergence. Reverted to scalar `axpy2_minus_unroll4_nofma` body
  pending a custom 4-dst-column 2-src kernel.
