## Decisions Made
- Gap detection gated on `0 < g < n`. A gap at height 0 would
  falsely disconnect the sink side; a gap at height ≥ n is
  meaningless (the tracked range is 0..n).
- Lifted vertices with excess are re-inserted into `bucket[n+1]`
  after a gap. Otherwise stranded preflow never returns to source
  and excess[sink] can report less than the true max-flow.
- Parallel edges kept separate. `(u, v, 3)` + `(u, v, 5)` produces
  two residual edges with caps 3 and 5 rather than one with cap 8.
  Consumers (K3, K4) may have reasons to inject multiple edges.
- `i64` capacities. Over-provisioned for K3/K4's small integer
  weights but costs nothing.
- Global relabeling deferred. Beneficial on large networks but
  our K3/K4 bands are small (100-5000 vertices); revisit at K6.
