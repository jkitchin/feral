## Decisions Made

- **Top-level compression in feral-scotch happens at the entry point,
  not per recursion frame.** Rationale: SCOTCH's compression is most
  effective on the full input where supervariables are abundant
  (structured KKT, FE meshes); at recursion depth the subgraph rarely
  has indistinguishable vertices left, and re-running the hash pass
  costs `O(|E|)` per frame for negligible payoff. Recorded inline in
  `node_nd.rs` module docs.

- **`feral-scotch` uses `feral-metis::internals` for the multilevel
  framework.** Avoids ~540 lines of duplication (coarsen, initial
  partition, RNG). The `internals` module is `#[doc(hidden)]` and
  carries no semver guarantee, which is the right tradeoff between
  reuse and crate independence. Recorded in feral-metis lib.rs and
  feral-scotch Cargo.toml.

- **Metis `MetisOptions` is constructed inside SCOTCH's
  `multilevel_node_bisection` to drive the shared coarsener.** The
  fields map cleanly: `n_sep_trials → niparts`, `fm_pass_cap →
  fm_passes`, `amd_switch → nd_to_amd_switch`,
  `coarsen_floor → coarsen_floor`. Two-hop ratio is hard-coded to 0.85
  (same value as METIS) since both papers cite it.
