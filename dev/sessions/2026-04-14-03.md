big-front tail — it is distributed evenly across the small-matrix
regime, consistent with a fixed per-matrix overhead dominated by
symbolic/allocation/assembly rather than the numeric kernel.

### Phase 2.5 profile — column-counts hypothesis overturned

Wrote `examples/profile_sparse_smallfront.rs` that inlines the
`symbolic_factorize` pipeline with per-phase `Instant::now()` timing
and runs over every KKT matrix with `n ≤ 500` and a MUMPS oracle
sidecar, filtering to `max_front < 200`. Goal: verify the plan's
Phase 2.5.1 hypothesis (Liu row-subtree column counts is "probably
the highest-leverage item") before spending 4–6 hours implementing
Liu's algorithm.

Result across 152128 small-frontal matrices:

```
Phase share (μs, percentage of total):
  total      9,376,324  100.0%
  symbolic   6,714,929   71.6%
    mc64       288,039    3.1%
    amd      3,733,092   39.8%  ← dominant
    etree    1,794,829   19.1%
    colcnt     242,495    2.6%  ← 15× smaller than amd
    snode      410,403    4.4%
  numeric    2,661,395   28.4%

Per-phase percentiles (μs):
  phase    p50  p90  p99   max
  mc64       0    5   23   109
  amd        0   28  554  9322  ← catastrophic fat tail
  etree      2   29  127   880
  colcnt     0    5   31   157
  snode      0    3   49   502
  numeric    1   55  253  1451
  total      5  145 1420 11257
```

**The column-counts hypothesis is wrong.** Column counts is 2.6% of
the small-frontal budget; a perfect Liu rewrite would shave at most
2.6 percentage points off the sparse ratio. The real target is
`amd_order`, which dominates at 39.8% and has a fat tail of ~9ms on
n=234 matrices.

**Top-30 offenders.** The small-frontal tail is dominated by four
families:

- **DISCS** (n=234, 20 matrices in top 30): AMD alone 9000–9300 μs,
  feral total ≈ 11000 μs, MUMPS ≈ 440 μs, ratio ≈ 25. **AMD alone is
  20× slower than MUMPS's entire analyse+factor** on this n=234
  family.
- **DMN15103** (n=99): AMD 1500–1800 μs, feral ≈ 2100, MUMPS ≈ 120,
  ratio ≈ 17. AMD is ~75% of feral work.
- **LAKES** (n=324): AMD 8200–8600, feral ≈ 11000, MUMPS ≈ 600,
  ratio ≈ 18. AMD is ~75% of feral work.
- **GROUPING** (n=225): *different pattern* — AMD only 750–810 μs,
  but snode 450+ μs (unusually large) and numeric 360+ μs, ratio ≈
  16. A separate follow-up item.

A one-hour profile saved 4–6 hours chasing the wrong optimization.
