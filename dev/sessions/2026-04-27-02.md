### Fix B: dense 2x2 BK inertia uses trace, not a00
- `src/dense/factor.rs::count_2x2_inertia` was deciding eigenvalue signs
  from sign of `a00` alone. KKT 2x2 blocks routinely have a00=0 (variable
  rows have zero Hessian diagonal). Switched to `trace = a00 + a11` per
  Sylvester's law -- matches `count_2x2_inertia_val` (sparse path),
  rmumps's `compute_inertia`, and canonical Fortran MUMPS.
- Threaded `a11` through `do_2x2_pivot` -> `count_2x2_inertia`.
- Re-attempt of the 2026-04-12 ACOPP30 fix that was abandoned at the time
  (regressed 16 dense matrices vs rmumps); canonical Fortran MUMPS later
  confirmed trace-based was correct and rmumps was wrong on those
  matrices. All 162 lib tests pass post-fix.
- Commit `5ecbeb5`.

### Fix A: bench prefers MUMPS oracle inertia over IPOPT iter-0 sidecar
- The bench's "inertia mismatch" headline previously compared feral's
  factorization against the IPOPT iter-0 `.json` sidecar's `inertia`
  field, which is *IPOPT's expected inertia at iteration entry* (n_x
  positives + n_eq+n_ineq negatives) -- NOT what MUMPS actually computed
  on the dumped matrix. On restoration-phase iterates and any case where
  MUMPS adds delta-x or delta-c regularization the two disagree.
- Regenerated 3,377 `.mumps.json` oracle files to record the
  MUMPS-computed inertia alongside `factor_us`/`solve_us`/`factor_nnz`
  (11,748 sidecars total now have inertia).
- Added `inertia: Option<(usize, usize, usize)>` to `OracleTiming` and
  threaded through both dense and sparse bench paths.
- Commit `174ea43`.

## Decisions Made
- **Memory HWM and Phase 2.8.1 slowness shared a root cause.** The
  `pick_default_method` extension to MetisND on low-degree large-n
  matrices fixed both, with no test regressions. The carried-forward
  "pivoting investigation" is closed by this commit.
- **Adopt MUMPS-computed inertia as the bench's ground truth.** The
  IPOPT iter-0 `.json` sidecar's `inertia` field stays as a fallback
  for sidecars without `.mumps.json`. Documented in commit message
  and the research note.
- **Do NOT adopt faer as a workspace dependency.** Pure-Rust
  clean-room constraint plus `pulp/dyn-stack/equator/reborrow/spindle`
  surface-area expansion outweigh the convenience. Adopt techniques
  only.
