### Phase 2.4.4 dense-tail investigation

- Wrote `src/bin/diag_dense_tail.rs` testing 5 `SupernodeParams` configs
  on 8 worst matrices. Key findings (committed as 332f23a):
  - Giant-frontal hypothesis REFUTED. Worst-matrix supernodes are
    ncol=14–18, not ~n. No single dense block.
  - `OrderingPreprocess::LdltCompress` is a big lever on the tail:
    HAHN1_0153 5867→1048μs, GAUSS2_0029 905→416μs (2–5×).
  - `nemin=5` (MUMPS default) is slower than `nemin=32` in feral.
  - CRESC100 is an exception — compression does not help.
- "Dense" bench column uses `factor_single_front` (`src/dense/factor.rs:447`),
  a whole-matrix dense LDLT with no symbolic phase. `SupernodeParams`
  changes have zero effect on it. The 53× max compares feral's dense
  `O(n³)` kernel against MUMPS's sparse multifrontal — not
  apples-to-apples and not a meaningful target for rook/ordering work.

### First flip attempt (rejected)

- Flipped `SupernodeParams::default().preprocess` to `LdltCompress`.
  Full 154,588-matrix bench regressed all three sparse metrics:
  geomean 0.36→0.49 (+36%), p90 1.61→1.91 (+19%), max 9.40→12.93
  (+38%). Reverted and documented in `dev/tried-and-rejected.md`
  (commit d0a4c0b).
- Root cause: `diag_compression_bench` times symbolic and numeric
  separately, reports only the numeric ratio. The real bench
  (`src/bin/bench.rs:1281-1284`) combines symbolic + numeric in
  `factor_us`. Compression roughly doubles symbolic time per matrix.

### Cache refactor + second flip attempt (cache kept, flip rejected)

- Wrote `src/bin/diag_compress_profile.rs` to time each step of the
  compression pipeline. Result: MC64 Hungarian is 70–97% of
  compression symbolic overhead. HAHN1_0153: mc64 283μs of 402μs
  total. KIRBY2_0007: 679μs of 698μs.
- Implemented MC64 caching refactor (commit eea9f19):
  - Split `mc64::compute_symmetric` into `compute_matching` (the
    expensive Hungarian + cost graph) and `scaling_from_cache` (O(n)).
  - Added `Mc64Cache` (perm, u, v, cmax, n_matched) stashed on
    `SymbolicFactorization::cached_mc64` when `LdltCompress` runs.
  - New `compute_scaling_with_cache` in `src/scaling/mod.rs`. Consumes
    the cache when the strategy resolves to `Mc64Symmetric` (directly
    or via `Auto`); falls back to the regular path otherwise. Policy 4
    fallback preserved.
  - `factorize_multifrontal` (sequential + parallel) pass the cache.
- Re-flipped and re-ran full bench. Cache recovered ~71% of the `max`
  gap and ~55% of `p90` but only ~8% of `geomean` — because the cache
  only helps matrices where `Auto` picks MC64. On the ACOPR30 family
  (9 of top-10 worst post-flip at ~9.5×), `diag_only/n < 0.3` so Auto
  picks InfNorm and the compression MC64 has no sharing partner.
- Revert the default flip. Cache refactor kept — it is a correct and
  legitimate speedup on the opt-in `LdltCompress` + MC64-scaling path.
