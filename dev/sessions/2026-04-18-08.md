### Priority 2: VESUVIO outlier diagnosed, deferred (commit 86cf1e8)

- New `src/bin/vesuvio_diag.rs` mirrors session 07's `cresc_diag`
  pattern. Reports symbolic shape, numeric factor time, actual
  max_nrow, total delays, root frontal size, and matrix-shape
  features (max_col_nnz, diag_only rows, zero diags) under both
  AMD and MetisND.

Key finding: not an ordering problem. Both AMD and MetisND produce
a 67%-of-n root frontal on every VESUVIO sample (single dense
linking column with 1026 nnz forces an unavoidable ~2060×960 dense
root). MetisND saves ≤8% on two samples and is *slower* on three.

Bottleneck is `src/dense/factor.rs` — scalar rank-1 BK updates,
no SIMD, no blocking. Cost analysis matches: 2059×959 ≈ 1.9 GFLOPs
at our ~8 GFLOP/s scalar ≈ 240ms (matches 236ms observed). MUMPS
DGETRF on Accelerate ≈ 400 GFLOP/s ≈ 5ms (matches 2.5ms oracle).
The 50× kernel gap explains the 84× factor ratio.

Implication: closing VESUVIO requires blocked BK + SIMD in
`src/dense/factor.rs`. Multi-session engineering, not addressable
this session. Corpus geomean unaffected; VESUVIO is tail
pathology.

## Decisions Made

- **`pick_default_method` will not route any pattern to KahipND.**
  Bake-off evidence shows KaHIP+K1 ties METIS on fill at 4-6× the
  per-call cost. Pinned by `pick_default_method_never_returns_kahip`
  test. Documented in `OrderingMethod::KahipND` docstring with
  cross-reference to `dev/research/ordering-kahip-driver-integration.md`.
  KaHIP remains reachable via `symbolic_factorize_with_method` and
  via `OrderingMethod::Auto`.

- **VESUVIO factor-time gap is a dense-kernel limitation, not an
  ordering limitation.** Both AMD and MetisND produce the same
  ~67%-of-n dense root frontal. Recorded in
  `dev/journal/2026-04-18-08.org` second entry; no code change
  this session. Closing the gap is deferred to a future
  blocked-BK + SIMD effort in `src/dense/factor.rs`.

## Abandoned Approaches

- **Routing VESUVIO to MetisND via a new dispatcher rule.**
  Diagnostic showed MetisND is 5-8% better on two of five samples
  and slower on three. Not worth a rule.
- **Adding a narrow KaHIP rule (option B in the research note).**
  No measured fill win on the IPM corpus and per-call setup cost
  is 3-7× AMD's on n<1000 matrices.
