## Abandoned Approaches

`mc64_resistant_n` initially built as `A = I + α u u^T` with
`u = 𝟙/√n` and `α = -2`. That gives eigenvalues 1 (multiplicity n-1)
and -1, so cond(A) = 1 and there is nothing for MC64 to fail at.
Verified: `np.linalg.cond(A) = 1.48` before symmetric row-max scaling,
unchanged after. The "rank-1 perturbation" framing was wrong — a
rank-1 update of a flat diagonal doesn't create dispersed
ill-conditioning. Switched to `A = Q D Q^T` with one eigenvalue at
`1e-8` and dense Q, which puts the ill-conditioning in a basis
direction that diagonal scaling cannot reach (`cond = 2e8` before
and after scaling). Documented in
`dev/research/synthetic-generators-m4.md` §4 and
`dev/tried-and-rejected.md`.
