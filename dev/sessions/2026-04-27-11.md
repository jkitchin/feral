## Accomplished

### Root cause: forest-Schur-etree breaks postorder tail identity

`schur_constrained_postorder` was emitting Schur nodes via DFS over
the Schur subtree. That works only when the Schur etree is a single
ascending chain; when the Schur cols form a *forest* (multiple
roots) plus internal Schur chains, DFS visits Schur nodes in
tree-walk order, not etree-index order, breaking the
`post[k] == k` tail identity that
`symbolic_factorize_with_schur` relies on.

Concrete on ACOPP30_0000 (n=209, n_schur=52): Schur roots were
{158, 159, 160, 161, 167, 168, 195, 196, 197, 203, 204} plus a
chain ending at 208. DFS broke the identity from col 174 onward —
the original A[174, 174] = -28.56 ended up at permuted (184, 184),
the diagonal at (174, 174) was reassigned to A[169, 169] = -0.1,
and the Schur block came out with max_rel = 0.997.

The release-mode silence is significant: the defensive
`debug_assert_eq!(post[k], k, ...)` at `src/symbolic/mod.rs:807`
caught this in debug-mode tests but was a no-op in release builds.

### Fix

`src/ordering/postorder.rs` `schur_constrained_postorder`:
phase-1 DFS over the whole etree emits non-Schur nodes only
(treating Schur nodes as transparent recursion points so non-Schur
descendants are reached), then phase-2 emits Schur nodes directly
in ascending etree-index order. This guarantees the tail identity
regardless of Schur-etree topology.

Why this is still a valid postorder: every Schur node's Schur
children have smaller etree index (parent[j] > j is the etree
invariant), so they are emitted earlier; non-Schur descendants
emitted in phase 1 sit at positions `< n_f`.
