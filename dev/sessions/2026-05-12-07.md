Decision: re-scope #13 explicitly (declare criterion #2 unreachable via
this lever) and unblock #9. Per-front kernel cost (32×32 SIMD kernel) is
the next plausible lever for the bench-ratio gap.

## Decisions Made

**Phase C scope narrowed to contrib-only single-slot.** Lifecycle
analysis of `FrontalFactors` fields: `l`, `d_diag`, `d_subdiag`,
`perm`, `perm_inv` are long-lived (move into `NodeFactors`), so they
have no recycle point. Only `contrib` has a natural recycle point
(post-`extend_add`), and only the multifrontal driver creates that
recycle opportunity. Pooling the long-lived fields would require an
ABI break or a `take_into_factors` swap; both deferred and likely
unnecessary given Phase C is bench-neutral.

**Single-slot, not multi-slot.** Empirically determined: see Abandoned
Approaches below and tried-and-rejected.

**Re-scope issue #13.** Criterion #2 (bench p90 small<1.30 OR
medium<1.60) is unreachable via allocation pooling. The contrib
malloc was not the bottleneck, and no further pooling lever exists.
Per-front kernel cost (32×32 SIMD) is the next plausible lever — that
is issue #9, which gates #10 (APP) and #11. Unblock #9.

## Abandoned Approaches

**Phase C multi-slot (Vec<Vec<f64>>).** Stack-of-buffers approach
preserved bit-parity but regressed bench p90 by ~+0.19 (small) /
~+0.30 (medium) in 4 consecutive runs. Cause: growable-indirection
bookkeeping (push/pop, scattered heap pointers) costs more than the
malloc/free pairs it avoided. Replaced by single-slot
`Option<Vec<f64>>` which restored A+B baseline. Recorded in
`dev/tried-and-rejected.md`.
