### 2.5.4 closed as documented-vacuous

An exhaustive grep shows `factor_nnz_estimate` / `factor_slack`
live only in `SymbolicFactorization` metadata, consumed by
`bench_orderings` and `hs85_diag` printers plus three "nonzero"
test asserts. The value does **not** drive preallocation of any
buffer in the factorization hot path:

- `NodeFactors.frontal_factors.l` — populated inside
  `factor_frontal_blocked`; no slack consulted.
- `ContribBlock.data` — cloned from kernel's `ff.contrib` per
  supernode; no pool, no preallocation.
- `FactorWorkspace.{frontal_values,dense_values,row_map,...}` —
  grow on demand per-supernode; no `factor_nnz_estimate`-sized
  field.

The named lever (adaptive slack) therefore has zero hot-path
leverage. Improving it would be a refactor of how
`FrontalFactors` owns its numeric storage (Phase 3 pooled
factor arena), not a 2-4h Phase 2.5 tweak. Closed as documented.

### 2.5.2 — Rayon parallelism: deferred with concrete plan

Research: `dev/research/phase-2.5.2-rayon-assembly-tree.md`.
Plan: `dev/plans/phase-2.5.2-rayon-assembly-tree.md` (Steps A-E).

Honest scoping: the Phase 2 planning doc's 4-8h estimate is
accurate. A correct parallel driver needs:

- Per-task `FactorWorkspace` (5 mutably-shared fields in the
  supernode body — cannot share across threads).
- Extraction of the 200-line per-supernode body into a
  reusable `factor_one_supernode()` helper (Step B).
- Task-graph parallel driver with atomic-counter readiness
  and `rayon::scope` (Step C).
- FP determinism: `extend_add` scatter order affects inertia
  on ill-conditioned matrices. Parallel sibling order must be
  made stable (sort `snode.children` by `snode_idx` before
  scatter — called out in the plan).
- Threshold gate to avoid overhead on small matrices.
- 154k-matrix corpus bit-exact parity cross-check.

Not safely doable in this session after the 2.5.x/2.5.4 work
already committed. Rushing the refactor would likely regress
the 154k-matrix correctness baseline — the explicit project
rule "correctness before performance, always". Deferred with a
concrete Step A-E plan that the next session can pick up.
