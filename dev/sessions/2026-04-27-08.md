## Decisions Made

- **Parallel concrete `*_amf` loops, not generic-ified AMD loops.**
  Considered making `select_pivot`, `create_element`, `finalize_step`,
  `run_elimination_amd` generic over `M: Metric` and dispatching the
  six AMF sites through trait calls. Rejected because (a) the AMD
  bit-parity contract (12/12 oracle_match SuiteSparse) is the
  load-bearing test for the entire ordering crate, and any change to
  AMD codegen risks regressing it; (b) trait-call inlining for the
  hot-path `bucket` dispatch is hard to audit without examining
  release-mode disassembly; (c) the duplication is ~300 LoC of
  copy-paste with localised metric-specific edits — a maintenance
  cost, but not a correctness risk. Once the AMF path has its own
  oracle gate (Phase C) and a Phase D corpus pass, generic-ification
  can be revisited cheaply with both bit-parity tests in place.

- **`wf` allocation lives on `Workspace`, not on the Metric trait.**
  Considered an associated type or a parallel `MetricState<M>` struct
  to keep AMD's memory footprint exactly identical. Rejected because
  4 bytes × n is negligible compared to the existing PE/IW/LEN/NV
  arrays and the cleanliness of "one workspace, two loops" wins. AMD
  never reads or writes the field.

- **`nv[me]` matches AMD's bookkeeping (`= nvpiv`), not MUMPS's
  (`= nvpiv + degme`).** Decided to keep AMD's convention because
  `finalize_permutation` is shared and uses `nv[i] > 0` to identify
  pivots and accumulates block sizes via `nv`. Recorded inline in
  the `finalize_step_amf` doc.
