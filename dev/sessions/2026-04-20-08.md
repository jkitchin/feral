### Microbench — `cargo bench --bench schur_kernel`

Key measurement at L = 256 on aarch64 NEON:

| variant                | time (ns) | speedup vs scalar |
|------------------------|----------:|------------------:|
| scalar                 |     92.00 |             1.00× |
| pulp (via Arch)        |    170.50 |             0.54× |
| direct_neon (FMA)      |     42.49 |             2.17× |
| unroll4_neon (FMA)     |     28.54 |             3.22× |
| **unroll4_nofma_neon** | **33.72** |         **2.73×** |

The wired-in production variant (`unroll4_nofma_neon`) hits the
plan's ≥2× gate at L=256 with 2.73×. It uses explicit
`simd.mul_f64s` + `simd.sub_f64s` (no FMA) for bit-exactness with
scalar rounding per the 2026-04-14 decision
(`dev/decisions.md` Phase 2.4.3 entry).

pulp-via-Arch dispatches slower than scalar at L=256 because of
runtime `__rust_pulp_rt_target_feature_detection` call overhead on
every invocation. That's why the kernel uses the direct const
`Neon::new_unchecked()` pattern (aarch64 always has NEON, no
dispatch needed). The pulp Arch path remains the cross-platform
fallback.
