Ghysels, Li, Rouet, Williams, and Napov (2016) describe STRUMPACK's
HSS-structured [[file:../concepts/multifrontal-method.org][multifrontal]]
solver, which uses Hierarchically Semi-Separable (HSS) compression with
randomized sampling citep:ghysels2016hss. This represents the hierarchical
counterpart to the flat BLR approach described in
[[file:./amestoy2017-blr-complexity.org][Amestoy et al. (2017)]].

** HSS Compression

HSS is a hierarchical low-rank format that recursively subdivides a dense
matrix and represents off-diagonal blocks at each level with low-rank
factorizations. Unlike BLR (which uses a single level of block partitioning),
HSS achieves asymptotically optimal complexity for matrices arising from
elliptic PDEs: O(n) for both factorization and solve in 2D.

The paper uses randomized sampling with interpolative decompositions to
construct the HSS representation efficiently. Random vectors are multiplied
against the frontal matrix, and the resulting samples reveal the rank structure
of off-diagonal blocks without computing them explicitly.

** Performance Results

STRUMPACK achieves up to 7x speedup over standard multifrontal factorization
for suitable problems (elliptic PDEs with smooth Green's functions). The
speedup increases with problem size because the low-rank property becomes more
pronounced for larger frontals. For problems without low-rank structure (e.g.,
highly indefinite systems), HSS compression provides little benefit and may
even slow down the factorization due to compression overhead.

** Relevance to FERAL

HSS compression is a potential Phase 4+ extension for FERAL, representing the
"optimal complexity" end of the low-rank spectrum. For FERAL's primary target
(KKT systems from NLP), the applicability of HSS depends on the structure of
the Hessian and Jacobian: smooth problems (continuous PDE discretizations)
benefit significantly, while combinatorial or highly nonlinear problems may not.

The key architectural insight from STRUMPACK is that low-rank compression can
be integrated into the multifrontal framework at the frontal matrix level,
which is exactly the boundary where FERAL's =DenseKernel= trait operates.
A future =CompressedDenseKernel= could implement BLR or HSS compression
behind the same trait interface.
