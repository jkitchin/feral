Pacaud, Shin, Schanen, Maldonado, and Anitescu (2023) present an approach to
accelerating interior point methods on GPU architectures by condensing the KKT
system into a dense matrix proportional to the degrees of freedom
citep:pacaud2023condensed. This is a fundamentally different approach to KKT
system solving than FERAL's sparse direct factorization.

** Condensed Approach

Instead of factoring the full sparse KKT matrix, the condensed approach
eliminates variables algebraically to produce a smaller, dense system. For
problems with structure (e.g., optimal power flow, where the number of control
variables is much smaller than the total state dimension), the condensed system
is small enough to factor as a dense matrix on a GPU.

This trades the sparsity-exploiting advantages of
[[file:../concepts/multifrontal-method.org][multifrontal factorization]] for
the raw throughput of dense GPU kernels operating on a smaller system. The
approach is effective when the condensed system is sufficiently small (typically
when the problem has few degrees of freedom relative to constraints).

** Relevance to FERAL

This paper represents an alternative architectural direction that FERAL
explicitly does not take. FERAL's design philosophy is "one engine" handling all
size regimes through the multifrontal framework, while the condensed approach is
specialized to problems with exploitable structure.

However, the paper's analysis of GPU dense kernel performance on KKT-derived
systems informs FERAL's Phase 4 GPU offload design. The threshold for GPU
offload of individual frontal matrices (~128x128 in FERAL's spec) is related to
the same crossover point where GPU throughput exceeds CPU throughput for dense
factorization.
