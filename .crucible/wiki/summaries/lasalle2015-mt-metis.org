LaSalle and Karypis (2015) present mt-Metis, a multithreaded nested dissection
framework for [[file:../concepts/sparse-matrix-ordering.org][fill-reducing
orderings]] on multicore architectures citep:lasalle2015mtmetis.

** Nested Dissection vs AMD

While [[file:./amestoy1996-amd.org][AMD]] is a local heuristic (greedy minimum
degree selection), nested dissection takes a global view: it recursively
bisects the graph using small vertex separators, producing an ordering where the
separator vertices are eliminated last. For regular 2D and 3D meshes, nested
dissection provably achieves asymptotically optimal fill. For irregular graphs,
the quality depends on the graph partitioner's ability to find balanced, small
separators.

** mt-Metis Results

mt-Metis achieves 1.5x faster ordering computation than ParMetis while
producing orderings with 3.7% fewer nonzeros and 14% fewer floating-point
operations in the subsequent factorization. On 16 cores, mt-Metis achieves up
to 10x speedup over serial Metis with only 1% increase in fill. The ordering
quality is preserved because mt-Metis parallelizes the coarsening and
refinement phases of the multilevel partitioning framework without sacrificing
partition quality.

** Relevance to FERAL

FERAL plans to add METIS ordering as a Phase 2 alternative to AMD. This paper
provides the state of the art in parallel METIS implementations. For FERAL's
target problem sizes (n ~ 2k-50k for the core target, up to 500k for Phase 3),
the ordering phase is typically a small fraction of total solve time, so the
parallelism benefit is less critical than ordering quality.

The key insight for FERAL is that METIS ordering can produce significantly
better fill than AMD on irregular graphs (which arise from unstructured
constraint Jacobians in NLP problems). FERAL's Tier 3 benchmark matrices
include cases specifically designed to test AMD vs METIS ordering quality.
