Hogg and Scott (2013) address the problem of pivot selection within
[[file:../concepts/multifrontal-method.org][multifrontal factorization]] of
sparse symmetric indefinite matrices citep:hogg2013pivoting. The paper examines
threshold pivoting and delayed pivot strategies that adapt
[[file:../concepts/bunch-kaufman-pivoting.org][Bunch-Kaufman-type pivoting]] to
the sparse context.

** Threshold Pivoting

In the multifrontal context, strict Bunch-Kaufman pivoting can reject too many
pivots within a frontal matrix, leading to excessive delayed pivots that inflate
parent frontals and degrade performance. Threshold pivoting relaxes the
acceptance criteria: a pivot is accepted if it satisfies a threshold condition
relative to the current column or frontal, rather than the full BK growth bound.
This produces more accepted pivots per frontal at the cost of potentially larger
element growth.

The paper distinguishes two modes implemented in SPRAL/SSIDS: TPP (Threshold
Partial Pivoting), which uses a simple threshold test, and APP (Approximate
Pivot Protocol), which uses a more sophisticated blocked strategy with delayed
pivoting.

** Delayed Pivot Architecture

When a pivot is rejected within a frontal, the variable is delayed to the parent
frontal. The child's contribution block grows to include the delayed variable,
and the parent incorporates it during assembly. This gives the parent additional
numerical context (entries from other children) that may make the delayed pivot
acceptable. The paper analyzes the performance impact of delayed pivoting:
excessive delays increase fill-in and reduce the effectiveness of supernode
amalgamation.

** Relevance to FERAL

This paper is the bridge between FERAL's Stage 1 dense
[[file:../concepts/bunch-kaufman-pivoting.org][Bunch-Kaufman]] implementation
and the Phase 2 multifrontal pivot strategy. The threshold pivoting modes (TPP
and APP) are the algorithms implemented in SPRAL's =ldlt_tpp.cxx= and
=ldlt_app.cxx=, which are FERAL's primary code inspection references for sparse
pivoting.

FERAL's Phase 1b uses =ZeroPivotAction::ForceAccept= as a simpler approach
(accepting all pivots and flagging for iterative refinement), deferring the more
sophisticated threshold pivoting to Phase 2 where delayed pivoting
infrastructure exists. The =PivotStrategy= trait, when designed in Phase 2,
will need to support both TPP-like and APP-like modes.
