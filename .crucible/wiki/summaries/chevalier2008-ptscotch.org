Chevalier and Pellegrini (2008) present PT-SCOTCH, a distributed-memory
parallel extension of [[file:./pellegrini1996-scotch.org][SCOTCH]] for graph
ordering and partitioning via MPI citep:chevalier2008ptscotch.

** Parallel Nested Dissection

PT-SCOTCH parallelizes the nested dissection framework by distributing the
graph across MPI processes. The key insight: at the top levels of the nested
dissection recursion, the graph is large enough to benefit from distributed
computation. At deeper levels (smaller subgraphs), the work is switched to
sequential SCOTCH on a single process.

The parallel/sequential switchover point is a parameter that balances
communication overhead (favoring sequential) against parallelism (favoring
distributed). Typical switchover: when the subgraph fits on a single process
(fewer than N/P vertices, where N is total and P is process count).

** Parallel Coarsening

Distributed coarsening uses a two-phase matching protocol:
1. *Local matching*: each process matches vertices within its local partition
   using SCOTCH's standard heavy-edge matching
2. *Boundary matching*: vertices on partition boundaries are matched across
   processes using a distributed protocol that ensures no vertex is matched
   twice

The folding step distributes the coarsened graph to fewer processes as it
shrinks, maintaining load balance. When the graph is small enough, it is
centralized on a single process for the initial partition.

** Parallel Refinement

Distributed FM refinement operates on the partition boundary:
1. Each process computes gains for its local boundary vertices
2. A global communication phase identifies the best moves
3. Moves are applied and partition state is synchronized

The communication cost per refinement pass is O(|boundary|), making it
efficient when the boundary-to-interior ratio is small. Band graph
optimization restricts refinement to a narrow band around the separator,
further reducing communication.

** Comparison with ParMETIS

On the test matrices from the paper (regular and irregular meshes up to
10M vertices, 16--128 MPI processes):

- *Ordering quality*: PT-SCOTCH produces fill within 5% of sequential SCOTCH,
  which is competitive with METIS
- *Scaling*: PT-SCOTCH shows better strong scaling than ParMETIS beyond 64
  processes, attributed to SCOTCH's band-graph optimization reducing
  communication
- *Ordering time*: Comparable to ParMETIS on moderate core counts (16--64),
  faster on high core counts (>64)

** Relevance to FERAL

PT-SCOTCH is relevant for FERAL Phase 4 (distributed-memory multifrontal
factorization). When the matrix is distributed across MPI ranks, the ordering
must also be computed in parallel to avoid a serial bottleneck. PT-SCOTCH
provides a proven distributed ordering that integrates with SCOTCH's strategy
string system.

For Phase 3 (shared-memory parallelism), sequential SCOTCH or
[[file:./lasalle2015-mt-metis.org][mt-METIS]] are more appropriate because
they avoid MPI overhead.
