De Simone, di Serafino, Gondzio, Pougkakiotis, and Viola (2022) provide a
comprehensive review in /SIAM Review/ of how interior point methods handle KKT
systems for sparse optimization, including specialized preconditioning and
iterative refinement strategies citep:desimone2022sparseapprox.

** KKT System Solving Strategies

The paper surveys the full range of approaches for solving the KKT systems that
arise at each interior point iteration: direct factorization (the approach FERAL
implements), iterative methods with preconditioning, and hybrid approaches that
use an approximate factorization as a preconditioner for an iterative solver.

For the direct approach, the paper discusses the challenges of ill-conditioning
as the barrier parameter mu approaches zero: the constraint block diagonal
grows as O(1/mu), creating condition numbers that can reach 10^12 or more. This
motivates the [[file:../concepts/symmetric-indefinite-factorization.org][equilibration]]
strategy used in FERAL.

** Iterative Refinement

The paper covers iterative refinement in depth, distinguishing between
fixed-precision refinement (FERAL's approach: 3 steps of correction using the
existing factors) and mixed-precision refinement (using lower precision for the
factorization and higher precision for the residual computation). The analysis
shows that for well-conditioned problems, fixed-precision refinement converges
in 1-2 steps, matching FERAL's expectation.

** Relevance to FERAL

This paper provides the broader context for FERAL's role in the NLP solver
stack. While FERAL implements direct factorization, understanding the
alternatives (iterative methods, hybrid approaches) helps assess which problem
classes FERAL's approach handles well and where future extensions might be
needed.

The paper's analysis of conditioning trajectories across interior point
iterations directly informs FERAL's benchmark design: the Tier 1 KKT matrices
span the full trajectory from well-conditioned (early iterations) to severely
ill-conditioned (convergence).
