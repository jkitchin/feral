This is the foundational paper for
[[file:../concepts/bunch-kaufman-pivoting.org][Bunch-Kaufman pivoting]]
citep:bunch1977stable. Bunch and Kaufman (1977) present stable methods for
calculating the inertia of symmetric matrices and for solving symmetric
indefinite linear systems using LDLt factorization with 1x1 and 2x2 pivot
blocks.

** Key Contributions

Theorem 3 gives the Bunch-Kaufman pivot selection algorithm. At each step of
the factorization, the algorithm examines the active submatrix and selects
either a 1x1 or a 2x2 pivot block. The selection compares the largest
off-diagonal element in the pivot column against the diagonal using the
threshold parameter alpha.

Section 4 establishes the optimal threshold alpha = (1 + sqrt(17)) / 8,
approximately 0.6404, which minimizes the element growth factor. This value
balances numerical stability against the frequency of 2x2 blocks (smaller alpha
produces more 2x2 blocks and tighter growth bounds, but at higher computational
cost).

The paper proves that 1x1 and 2x2 blocks are both necessary and sufficient for
stable factorization of any nonsingular symmetric matrix. The existence theorem
guarantees that at every step of the elimination, either an acceptable 1x1
pivot or an acceptable 2x2 pivot can be found.

** Inertia and Sylvester's Law

The paper establishes the connection between the D blocks in the factorization
P*L*D*L^T*P^T = A and the inertia of A via Sylvester's Law. The inertia
(counts of positive, negative, and zero eigenvalues) can be read directly from
the D blocks without computing actual eigenvalues. This property is central to
FERAL's design, where exact inertia reporting is a hard constraint required by
the NLP solver POUNCE for convergence monitoring.

** Relevance to FERAL

This paper is required reading before implementing the dense
[[file:../concepts/symmetric-indefinite-factorization.org][LDLt factorization]].
Sections 2-4 contain all the algorithmic details needed for FERAL's Stage 1
scalar Bunch-Kaufman implementation. The exact test matrices for FERAL's test
suite are derived from examples in this paper.

FERAL's =BunchKaufmanParams= struct directly implements the parameters from this
paper: the alpha threshold, zero-pivot tolerance, and the three-way
=ZeroPivotAction= for handling numerically zero pivots.
