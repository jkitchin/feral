This survey by Davis, Rajamanickam, and Sid-Lakhdar (2016) is the definitive
modern reference covering the full landscape of sparse direct solvers
citep:davis2016survey. Published in /Acta Numerica/, it spans ordering,
symbolic analysis, numerical factorization, triangular solve, and software
across all matrix symmetry classes.

** Scope

The survey covers the complete pipeline of sparse direct solution: reordering
for fill reduction ([[file:../concepts/sparse-matrix-ordering.org][AMD]],
nested dissection, constrained orderings), symbolic factorization (elimination
trees, supernodes, column counts), numeric factorization (left-looking,
right-looking, [[file:../concepts/multifrontal-method.org][multifrontal]]),
and the solve phase. It treats LU, Cholesky, and LDLt factorizations, with
specific attention to the symmetric indefinite case relevant to FERAL.

** Key Insights for FERAL

The survey provides a unified view of how modern solvers (MUMPS, PARDISO,
CHOLMOD, SSIDS) make architectural decisions about supernode amalgamation,
memory management, and parallelism. It clarifies the tradeoffs between
left-looking and multifrontal approaches: left-looking has simpler memory
patterns but less parallelism, while multifrontal (FERAL's chosen approach)
enables tree-based parallelism and dense BLAS-3 kernels at the cost of the
assembly overhead.

The survey also covers the state of the art in fill-reducing orderings,
comparing AMD, METIS, and nested dissection on practical problem classes. This
context informs FERAL's decision to start with AMD and add METIS in Phase 2.
