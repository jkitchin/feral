Bunch and Parlett (1971) describe complete pivoting for symmetric indefinite
systems of linear equations citep:bunch1971direct. This paper predates the
Bunch-Kaufman algorithm and provides the theoretical ceiling for pivot stability
in symmetric factorization.

** Complete Pivoting

Complete pivoting searches the entire active submatrix (not just a single column)
for the best pivot element at each step. This produces the tightest element
growth bounds but at O(n^2) cost per pivot step (vs O(n) for
[[file:../concepts/bunch-kaufman-pivoting.org][Bunch-Kaufman]]). The paper
establishes the existence theorem: for any nonsingular symmetric matrix, a
stable 1x1 or 2x2 pivot always exists.

** Inertia Preservation

The paper provides the foundational proof that congruence transformations
(P*L*D*L^T*P^T = A) preserve inertia, which later became the basis for
Sylvester's Law application in the Bunch-Kaufman algorithm. This proof is
essential for FERAL's guarantee of exact inertia counting.

** Relevance to FERAL

While FERAL implements Bunch-Kaufman pivoting (partial pivoting) rather than
Bunch-Parlett complete pivoting, this paper provides the theoretical ceiling
against which BK's growth bounds are compared. It is also the source of the
existence theorem cited in the FERAL project spec. The paper is referenced in
FERAL's research notes as foundational theory, even though the algorithm itself
is not directly implemented.
