Scaling or /equilibration/ of a matrix A replaces A with a congruent matrix
$\tilde A = D_1 A D_2$ whose nonzeros are closer to unit magnitude. For
symmetric indefinite direct solvers (Bunch-Kaufman, rook, multifrontal
LDL^T), scaling is a pre-processing step that can dramatically reduce
delayed pivots, improve residual accuracy, and sometimes change the
numerical inertia question from intractable to well-conditioned
citep:ruiz2001.

Two orthogonal design axes matter for our use case in FERAL's
[[file:./symmetric-indefinite-factorization.org][symmetric indefinite
factorization]]:

1. *Symmetry preservation.* Since we apply scaling as a congruence
   $D A D$, the scaling vector must be identical on both sides.
   Algorithms that alternate row-then-column normalization (e.g.
   Sinkhorn-Knopp) break symmetry at each half-step and cannot be
   used directly.
2. *Target norm.* The ∞-norm (Ruiz 2001), 1-norm (Knight-Ruiz-Uçar
   2014 variant), and sum-of-log-squares objective (Curtis-Reid 1972)
   each produce different fixed points with different conditioning
   properties.

** Ruiz 2001: simultaneous row and column ∞-norm equilibration

The Ruiz 2001 algorithm iterates the step

$$A^{(k+1)} = D_R^{-1/2}\, A^{(k)}\, D_C^{-1/2}$$

where $D_R$ and $D_C$ are the diagonals of row/column ∞-norms of
$A^{(k)}$ citep:ruiz2001. On symmetric input $D_R = D_C$, so the
iterate preserves symmetry at every step — this is explicitly
called out in the paper §3 as the principal reason to prefer it over
Sinkhorn-Knopp for symmetric matrices.

Key properties (Ruiz 2001 §3):

- *Asymptotic linear convergence with rate 1/2.* Each iteration
  halves the distance to the fixed point of unit row/column ∞-norms.
- *After iteration 1, all entries satisfy $|\tilde a_{ij}| \le 1$.*
- *Single-iteration convergence on diagonally dominant matrices.*
  If $|a_{ii}| \ge |a_{ij}|$ and $|a_{ii}| \ge |a_{ji}|$ for all
  $j \ne i$, one sweep produces $\tilde a_{ii} = 1$.
- *Optimal conditioning.* By Bauer (1963) / van der Sluis (1969),
  equilibrated ∞-norms imply a bounded-above relationship for the
  condition number relative to the best possible diagonal scaling.

The FERAL implementation at ~src/scaling/infnorm.rs~ is labelled
"Knight-Ruiz" but is structurally Ruiz Algorithm 2.1 adapted to
Jacobi sweeps over CSC lower-triangle storage. A rename + research
note is scheduled (see ~dev/plans/scaling-algorithms-expansion.md~).

** Curtis-Reid 1972 / MC29: least-log-squares scaling

The Curtis-Reid scaling is the other classical "safe" choice. It
finds diagonal vectors $r$ and $c$ that minimize

$$\sum_{a_{ij} \ne 0} (\log|a_{ij}| + r_i + c_j)^2$$

by solving the associated least-squares normal equations with a
small number of conjugate gradient iterations citep:reid2023. The
resulting scaling makes the /logarithms/ of nonzeros as close to zero
as possible in a least-squares sense — a global objective, not a
per-row or per-column one.

From the HSL MC29 specification citep:reid2023:

#+BEGIN_QUOTE
To compute scaling factors $R$ and $C$ for a real sparse rectangular
matrix $A$ such that the scaled matrix $R^{-1} A C^{-1}$ has its
nonzeros near unity in the sense that the sum of the squares of the
logarithms of the moduli of the nonzeros is minimized.  Use of this
method gives far better results on sparse matrices than scaling to
equilibrate row norms.
#+END_QUOTE

MC30 is the symmetric variant (referenced in Ruiz 2001 §1) and is the
correct target for FERAL, where scaling is applied as a congruence.

Why "safe": Curtis-Reid has been in production at HSL since 1972.
It has no iteration-bound surprises (CG converges in ≤ rank steps
on the normal equations), preserves symmetry by construction in the
MC30 form, and tends to behave well precisely on matrices where
norm-balancing fails — such as FERAL's problem case MSS1_0009,
where the Ruiz infinity-norm iteration produces a matrix whose
scaled $\max|{\rm off}|/|{\rm diag}|$ ratio explodes to $\sim 10^{14}$
and motivates the Policy 4 fallback in ~src/scaling/mod.rs~.

** Matching-based: Duff-Koster 2001 / MC64

MC64 is a separate family: first find a permutation that maximizes
the product of absolute values on the diagonal (a maximum-weight
bipartite matching problem), then scale so those diagonal entries
are 1 and off-diagonals are ≤ 1. Already implemented in FERAL as
~src/scaling/mc64.rs~ via a pure-Rust Hungarian algorithm. See
also [[file:./symmetric-indefinite-factorization.org][symmetric
indefinite factorization]] for how the matching interacts with
pivoting and the arrow-KKT shape heuristic that routes FERAL's
~Auto~ strategy.

*** Bertsekas auction: a parallel alternative to Hungarian

The Hungarian algorithm has an O(n³) worst case and is serial. The
Bertsekas auction algorithm solves the same assignment problem via
ε-complementary-slackness bidding citep:bertsekas2001. Key features:

- *Polynomial complexity* with ε-scaling (geometric reduction of ε
  across outer iterations).
- *Massively parallelizable* — each "person" can bid
  independently in a round (Bertsekas 2001, §on "Parallel and
  Asynchronous Implementation").
- *Same optimal assignment* as Hungarian (within ε-CS tolerance);
  the final ε is driven small enough that the result is
  integer-optimal for integer-weight problems.

FERAL does not currently need auction — the Hungarian is not on
the critical path of any bench matrix — but the ingested paper is
available for future parallelization of MC64.

** Sinkhorn-Knopp 1967: rejected for symmetric solvers

Sinkhorn and Knopp's iteration alternately normalizes row-sums
then column-sums to produce a doubly stochastic matrix. Ruiz 2001
§3 argues explicitly against it for symmetric inputs: alternating
row/column sweeps break symmetry at each half-step, and the
resulting scaling depends on whether A or A^T was the input.
For general unsymmetric scaling, Sinkhorn-Knopp is a reasonable
choice; for FERAL's symmetric indefinite context, it is not.

** Decision matrix for FERAL

| Algorithm            | Symmetric-preserving | Target norm   | Convergence rate            | Status in FERAL |
|----------------------+----------------------+---------------+-----------------------------+-----------------|
| Ruiz 2001 ∞-norm     | yes                  | ∞-norm        | linear, rate 1/2            | implemented (as ~infnorm.rs~); rename scheduled |
| Knight-Ruiz-Uçar 2014| yes                  | 1-norm        | linear (proved)             | deferred |
| Curtis-Reid 1972     | yes (MC30 variant)   | log-sum-sq    | CG, ≤ rank iters            | planned (safe candidate) |
| MC64 matching        | via symmetric-avg    | matching      | O(n³) worst case Hungarian  | implemented |
| Bertsekas auction    | n/a (matching-level) | matching      | polynomial with ε-scaling   | deferred |
| Sinkhorn-Knopp       | *no*                 | 1-norm        | geometric                   | rejected |

** Relationship to FERAL's ~ScalingStrategy~

The ~ScalingStrategy~ enum at ~src/scaling/mod.rs:86~ currently
exposes ~InfNorm~, ~Mc64Symmetric~, ~Identity~, ~External~, and
~Auto~. The expansion plan (~dev/plans/scaling-algorithms-expansion.md~)
will add ~Ruiz~ (renamed ~InfNorm~) and ~CurtisReid~, turning the
enum into the full decision matrix above.
